About

Robotics engineer with a mechanical foundation.

I'm a grad student at Northeastern's Silicon Synapse Lab, developing adaptive motor control systems under the PRANCE project. My Mechanical Engineering background gives me a hardware-first perspective that I pair with software skills in ROS2, computer vision, and deep learning.

Before grad school, I spent two years in industry — designing precision fixtures at ATE Enterprises (achieving 0.07 µm surface finishes) and doubling spindle output at TeraSpin Technologies. I bring that same precision mindset to every robotics system I build.

I'm actively seeking co-op and internship roles in robotics, mechatronics, and controls engineering. Open to full-time positions starting May 2027.

PROJECT 01 · FEATURED
Autonomous Navigation ROS2 SLAM

CANDI Rover — Autonomous Delivery Robot

Autonomous candy-delivery robot built in Gazebo/ROS2 using the Nav2 navigation stack and SLAM Toolbox. Maps its environment in real time, localizes within that map, and navigates to goal poses while avoiding dynamic obstacles.

  • 95% task success rate across 40 controlled simulation trials
  • Built Gazebo world from scratch with custom obstacle configurations
  • Integrated Nav2 with SLAM Toolbox for simultaneous mapping and localization
  • Authored IEEE-format final report and GitHub documentation

Drone Racing Trajectory Optimization

End-to-end pipeline for optimal drone racing trajectories using RRT/RRT* for path generation and GTSAM factor graphs + SciPy solvers for time-optimal trajectory refinement.

PROJECT 04 · GRADUATE RESEARCH
Motor Control Signal Processing Prosthetics

PRANCE — Adaptive Motor Control Research

Graduate research at the Silicon Synapse Lab characterizing motor response dynamics for adaptive prosthetic and robotics control. Applied the Differential Rise Method to measure electromechanical time constants with sub-millisecond precision.

  • Measured τ ≈ 20.3 ± 2.6 ms using Differential Rise Method
  • Designed motor testing protocols and data acquisition pipelines
  • Results inform adaptive control for next-generation prosthetics
